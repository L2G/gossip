All of the scripts do two things: they gather a scandal and its details from somewhere, then they send that information to one or more cronies. Decoding the cutesy metaphor:

  • A scandal is a short piece of text, like the Subject line of an email message.

  • The details is, usually, a longer piece of text, like the body of an email message. The distinction is important because some cronies, like Twitter, only get the scandal and not the details.

  • Cronies represent a destination for the information. Cronies can be sites like Twitter or Campfire, or they can be programs like email or Jabber.
